Preparing for your future with a workplace pension - what you need to know

If your future is important to you, consider enrolling into your workplace pension.

Pensions might seem complicated, but the basic idea is a simple one. It’s important to understand the benefits of contributing to a workplace pension, because your State Pension – while providing a foundation – may not be enough to live on in your future.

The importance of retirement savings

Millions of people aren’t saving nearly enough to give sufficient funds for living they hope for when they retire. Or, perhaps, it hasn’t been a thought for consideration and reluctantly, it has been left too late to start making pension contributions worthwhile.

A State Pension, offering £179.60 a week (tax year 2021/22), is what people eligible will receive when they retire. On average, you need at least 35 qualifying years to qualify for the full amount, which means if you are working on a temporary basis, it could take you a lot longer than the average worker to reach this.

What about Teacher’s Pension?

The Teacher’s Pension Scheme was introduced to England and Wales in 2007. The Teacher’s Pension Scheme is a government and employer-backed fund that make equal contributions to every contribution you make.

Unfortunately, agency supply teachers are not eligible for Teacher’s Pension. The best option for a supply teacher to contribute towards their future is to enrol in a workplace pension.

In April 2020 nearly 8 out of 10 employees (78%) had a workplace pension, with an 80% participation from employees aged 22 to 29 years old.

What are the benefits of a workplace pension?

When you are employed, your employer will enrol you into their workplace pension if you’re an eligible employee – this is called automatic enrolment and is made by law. You’ll be eligible if you’re:

  • Aged 22 or over
  • Under State Pension age
  • Earning more than £192 per week (or £833 per month/£10000 per year)
  • Working in the UK

When you make contributions into your pensions, your employer has to make a contribution into the scheme as well. You’ll also get tax relief on the contributions you make into the scheme.

Let’s look at an example of your total contributions

If you’re in a workplace pension, each payday you’ll contribute:

  • You put in £40
  • Your employer puts in £30
  • You get £10 tax relief

A total of £80 goes into your pension.

Your workplace pension is yours for life. If you work on a temporary basis, it is a good idea to look at where your contributions are and make continuous contributions into the same pension pot. The longer you save the more time there is for your money to grow, so the earlier you start contributing the better.

If you work on a temporary basis, and for multiple agencies, it is worth considering an umbrella company for your employer. With an umbrella company, you can make continuous contributions to a pension, so when it comes to retirement, all of your savings are in one place.
